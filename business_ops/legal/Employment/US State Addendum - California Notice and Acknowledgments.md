# State-Specific Employment Addendum — California

Created by: Travis Sheppard
Category: HR & Employment (US — CA)
Status: Template for Legal Review

This California Addendum (the “Addendum”) supplements the Employment Agreement for Employees
whose principal place of work is California. If there is a conflict, this Addendum controls to
the extent required by California law.

1) Governing Law; Venue
- California law governs California employment rights; venue for employment disputes lies in
  California courts as required by Cal. Lab. Code §925 (if applicable).

2) Wage Statement and Pay Requirements (Cal. Lab. Code §226; §204)
- Pay frequency: at least twice monthly on designated paydays.
- Wage statement will include all required items (gross wages, total hours for non‑exempt,
  piece‑rate units, all deductions, net wages, pay period dates, employee name/last 4 SSN,
  employer legal name/address, hourly rates, hours at each rate).

3) Meal and Rest Periods (IWC Wage Orders)
- Non‑exempt employees are entitled to compliant meal and rest periods. Company policy will
  provide details; premium pay will be provided where required.

4) Paid Sick Leave (Healthy Workplaces, Healthy Families Act; local ordinances)
- Accrual at no less than 1 hour per 30 hours worked; annual accrual/carryover and usage will
  comply with current state minimums (e.g., 40 hours/5 days) and any more generous local
  ordinances.

5) Overtime (Cal. Lab. Code §510)
- Overtime for non‑exempt: over 8 hours/day or 40 hours/week; double time as applicable.

6) Harassment Prevention Training (Gov. Code §12950.1)
- Mandatory training will be provided within required timelines.

7) Non‑Compete
- Post‑employment non‑compete covenants are void under Bus. & Prof. Code §16600. Company
  relies on confidentiality, trade secret, and non‑solicitation (to the extent permitted by
  law).

8) Arbitration/Claims
- Any arbitration or forum selection will comply with Cal. law and will not waive
  non‑waivable rights. PAGA/representative action rights addressed per current law.
