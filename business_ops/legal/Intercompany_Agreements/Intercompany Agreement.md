# Ai‑First Group – Intercompany and IP Transfer Agreement (Draft)

**Date:** [To be finalized]

**Parties:**

1. **Ai‑First Holdings Ltd.** (Cayman Islands) – the “Parent Company”

2. **Ai‑First Inc.** (Delaware, USA) – the “U.S. Subsidiary”

3. **Ai‑First Spain, S.L.** (Spain) – the “EU Subsidiary”

4. (Future) **Ai‑First Portugal Lda.** (Portugal) – the “Management Subsidiary”

Collectively referred to as the “Parties” or the “Group.”

---

## 1. Purpose

This agreement defines the framework for:
- Transfer and ownership of all intellectual property (IP) created by any entity within the Group.
- Intercompany service, licensing, and cost-sharing arrangements.
- Compliance with transfer pricing and tax regulations in each jurisdiction.

---

## 2. Intellectual Property Ownership

### 2.1 IP Assignment

All IP created by employees, contractors, or subsidiaries of the Group shall be **assigned to Ai‑First Holdings Ltd. (Cayman)** as the ultimate owner and custodian of intellectual property.

### 2.2 Scope of IP

Includes but is not limited to:
- Software source code, algorithms, AI models, and datasets.

- Brand names, trademarks, and trade dress.

- Business processes, documentation, and frameworks.

- Patents, designs, and confidential know-how.

### 2.3 Local Use and Licensing

Each Subsidiary is granted a **non-exclusive, royalty-bearing license** from the Parent Company to use the IP in its local operations, subject to:
- Compliance with Parent IP policy and security controls.

- Restriction on sublicensing without written approval.

- Obligation to report any local improvements or derivative works to the Parent.

### 2.4 Improvements

Any enhancements or derivatives made by a Subsidiary automatically vest in the Parent Company, with the Subsidiary retaining the right to use such improvements under its license.

### 2.5 Economic Substance Requirements (Cayman Islands)

To ensure compliance with the Cayman Islands Economic Substance Act 2018 (as amended), Ai‑First
Holdings Ltd. shall maintain:

#### 2.5.1 Adequate Resources
- Employees: Minimum one (1) qualified employee in the Cayman Islands with expertise in IP
  management, OR outsourced management via a registered Cayman service provider with adequate
  oversight by the Board.
- Office: Physical office space in the Cayman Islands (a serviced office is acceptable if used
  for Board meetings and CIGA activities).
- Expenditure: Annual operating expenditure proportionate to IP business income (target: 7–10%
  of royalty revenue), documented in the ESA file.

#### 2.5.2 Core Income Generating Activities (CIGA)
The following CIGA shall be performed in the Cayman Islands:
1) Board meetings held in Cayman (minimum two per year) for strategic IP decisions.  
2) Review and approval of the IP development roadmap.  
3) Negotiation and approval of material IP licensing agreements (over USD $100,000 value).  
4) Management of IP portfolio risks and IP protection strategy.  
5) Oversight of subsidiary R&D activities via quarterly reviews.

#### 2.5.3 Documentation and Reporting
- ESA Filing: Annual economic substance return filed with the Cayman Registrar by the required
  deadline.  
- Board Minutes: Cayman Board meetings documented with attendance records demonstrating
  in‑person/Cayman participation.  
- CIGA Evidence: Contemporaneous documentation of CIGA performed (meeting minutes, IP strategy
  documents, licensing negotiations, approvals).

#### 2.5.4 Responsibility
The Group CFO and the Cayman registered office provider are jointly responsible for ESA
compliance monitoring and filing.

## 3. Intercompany Services

### 3.1 Service Categories

Each Subsidiary will perform specific functions within the Group:

| Entity | Function | Cost Basis | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Cayman (HoldCo) | IP ownership, strategy, global finance | N/A | Receives royalties/licensing revenue |
| Delaware (US) | Product development, U.S. sales, and marketing | Cost + 7% markup | Provides R&D and customer support |
| Spain (EU) | EU operations, implementation, and client delivery | Cost + 7% markup | Supports EU clients and localization |
| Portugal (Mgmt) | Management and R&D (future) | Cost + 5% markup | Founder and leadership services |

### 3.2 Transfer Pricing

All intercompany transactions shall be conducted on an **arm’s-length basis**, in accordance with OECD Transfer Pricing Guidelines.

### 3.3 Cost Allocation

Shared costs (e.g., SaaS infrastructure, R&D tooling, marketing) shall be allocated based on revenue share, headcount, or usage metrics as determined annually.

---

## 4. Licensing and Royalty Structure

### 4.1 Royalties

Each Subsidiary will pay a royalty to Ai‑First Holdings Ltd. for the use of Group IP as follows:
- Standard royalty rate: **3–5% of gross revenue**, depending on activity type.
- Royalty adjustments subject to periodic review to maintain compliance with local tax laws.

### 4.2 Invoicing

Royalties shall be invoiced quarterly, with payment due within 30 days of invoice date.

## 6. Data Protection and Confidentiality

### 6.1 GDPR Compliance for Intercompany Data Transfers

6.1.1 Data Processing Agreement. For any processing of Personal Data (as defined in GDPR
Article 4) by one Group entity on behalf of another, the parties shall execute a Data
Processing Agreement (DPA) in the form of Annex A (EU Standard Contractual Clauses, 2021
version: Module 2 Controller→Processor or Module 3 Processor→Processor, as applicable).
Annex A shall include completed Annex I/II/III details (parties, descriptions of transfers,
and technical/organizational measures) at execution and be kept current.

6.1.2 Cross‑Border Transfers. All transfers of Personal Data from Ai‑First Spain, S.L. to
entities outside the EEA (including Ai‑First Inc. (USA) and Ai‑First Holdings Ltd. (Cayman))
shall be governed by the SCCs (incorporated by reference as Annex B) and supported by an
annual Transfer Impact Assessment (TIA) in accordance with Schrems II, documenting adequacy of
supplementary measures.

6.1.3 Permitted Data Categories. Permitted transfers subject to SCCs and TIA include: (i)
employee personal data (HR/admin); (ii) customer business contact data (CRM/sales support);
and (iii) de‑identified user behavioral data for AI model improvement. Transfers of special
categories of data (GDPR Art. 9) to Cayman are prohibited absent explicit consent and legal
basis.

6.1.4 Security Measures. Parties shall implement Article 32 GDPR technical and organizational
measures: encryption in transit and at rest, role‑based access controls and MFA, annual pen
tests, and incident response with 72‑hour breach notification to the DPO.

6.1.5 Breach Notification. Any Group entity discovering a personal data breach shall notify the
Spanish DPO within 24 hours. Ai‑First Spain, S.L. will notify AEPD within 72 hours per Article
33 GDPR and affected data subjects per Article 34 where required.

6.1.6 Data Subject Rights. Group entities shall cooperate to fulfill data subject requests
(access, rectification, erasure, portability, restriction, objection) within statutory
timelines.

### 6.2 Confidentiality (Non‑Personal Data)
All confidential business information shared between Parties shall be kept strictly
confidential and used only to perform obligations under this Agreement.

## 7. Term and Termination

This Agreement shall remain in effect until terminated by mutual consent or upon dissolution of one or more Parties.

Termination does not affect accrued obligations or IP ownership vested in the Parent.

## 8. Dispute Resolution

### 8.1 Governing Law
This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the Cayman
Islands, without regard to conflict of law principles.

### 8.2 Arbitration (LCIA)
8.2.1 Mandatory Arbitration. All disputes, controversies, or claims arising out of or relating
to this Agreement, including its formation, validity, breach, or termination, shall be finally
resolved by arbitration under the Rules of the London Court of International Arbitration
(LCIA), which rules are deemed incorporated by reference.

8.2.2 Seat and Language. Seat: London, United Kingdom. Language: English. Applicable law:
Cayman Islands substantive law.

8.2.3 Number of Arbitrators. Disputes under USD $500,000 shall be heard by a sole arbitrator;
disputes at or above that amount shall be heard by three arbitrators.

8.2.4 Arbitrator Qualifications. Arbitrator(s) shall have expertise in Cayman Islands corporate
law and international IP licensing. The parties may jointly request the LCIA Court to appoint
arbitrator(s) with Cayman law expertise.

8.2.5 Emergency Arbitrator and Expedited Formation. The parties agree to invoke the LCIA
Emergency Arbitrator and Expedited Formation provisions (LCIA Rules 2020, Articles 9B and 9C)
for urgent relief applications.

8.2.6 Confidentiality. All arbitration proceedings, documents, and awards shall be kept
strictly confidential, except as required for enforcement or by applicable law.

8.2.7 Multi‑Party Consolidation. If disputes involve multiple Group entities, the parties
consent to consolidation of related arbitrations under LCIA Rules Article 22, subject to
tribunal approval.

### 8.3 Equitable Relief Carve‑Out
Notwithstanding Section 8.2, any party may seek temporary or preliminary injunctive relief from
any court of competent jurisdiction to (i) prevent irreparable harm to Intellectual Property,
including unauthorized disclosure or infringement; (ii) enforce confidentiality obligations;
or (iii) prevent breach of non‑compete or non‑solicitation provisions. Such applications shall
not waive arbitration of the underlying dispute.

### 8.4 Enforcement
Any arbitral award may be enforced in the Cayman Islands Grand Court, U.S. District Courts,
Spanish courts, and courts of any jurisdiction in which a party has assets, under the New York
Convention.
