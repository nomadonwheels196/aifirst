### 3.1 Pre‑emption Rights on Transfers to Third Parties (art. 107 LSC)

**Scope**: Any proposed transfer of participations to a third party (other than transfers among group entities, which are permitted with Board approval, or transfers pursuant to inheritance or intra‑family succession) shall be subject to the following pre‑emption procedure:

**Notice**: The transferring socio (the "Transferor") shall deliver written notice to the Board and all other socios (the "Pre‑emption Notice") specifying: (i) the number of participations proposed to be transferred; (ii) the identity of the proposed transferee; (iii) the proposed price per participation and total consideration; and (iv) all material terms of the proposed transfer.

**Timeline**: Within thirty (30) calendar days from receipt of the Pre‑emption Notice, each socio and the Company shall have the right to notify the Transferor of their election to purchase all or a portion of the offered participations (the "Acceptance Notice"). Failure to deliver an Acceptance Notice within the 30-day period shall constitute a waiver of pre‑emption rights.

**Valuation**: The purchase price shall be the price specified in the Pre‑emption Notice, unless the Transferor and accepting socio(s) agree to independent valuation. If independent valuation is requested by any accepting party, it shall be conducted by an independent auditor or valuation firm jointly appointed by the Transferor and accepting parties within five (5) Business Days; failing agreement, the Board shall appoint the valuer. Valuation shall be completed within twenty (20) Business Days from appointment and shall be binding on all parties.

**Allocation Waterfall (Simplified)**: If aggregate Acceptance Notices exceed the number of participations offered, the participations shall be allocated as follows:
1. **First**, pro rata to all accepting socios based on their respective ownership percentages immediately prior to the proposed transfer (calculated as: [socio's ownership %] ÷ [total ownership % of all accepting socios] × [number of participations offered]); and
2. **Second**, any remaining participations (if any, due to rounding or partial acceptances) to the Company, if the Company has elected to purchase.

If the Company has not elected to purchase remaining participations, such participations shall revert to the Transferor for sale to the third party as set forth in "Failure of Pre-emption" below.

**Payment Terms**: The accepting parties shall pay the aggregate purchase price within thirty (30) calendar days from delivery of the Acceptance Notice (or, if independent valuation is obtained, within thirty (30) calendar days from receipt of the valuation report). Payment shall be made in cash (or by wire transfer to the account designated by the Transferor) against delivery of the share certificates and execution of share transfer instruments in the form required by the Registro Mercantil.

**Closing and Registration**: Upon receipt of payment, the Transferor shall deliver executed share transfer forms and any other documents required by the Board or the Registro Mercantil. The transfer shall be recorded in the Libro de Socios and reported to the Registro Mercantil in accordance with LSC formalities.

**Failure of Pre‑emption**: If no Acceptance Notices are delivered within the 30-day period (or if accepting parties fail to consummate the purchase within the payment period), the Transferor may complete the transfer to the third party identified in the Pre‑emption Notice on terms no more favorable than those set forth in the Pre‑emption Notice, within ninety (90) calendar days thereafter; provided that such transfer remains subject to Board approval as required by LSC Article 107 and these Estatutos.

**Transfers Among Group Entities**: Notwithstanding the foregoing, transfers among entities within the Ai‑First group (being Ai‑First Holdings Ltd. (Cayman), Ai‑First Inc. (Delaware), and Ai‑First Spain, S.L.) are permitted transfers and not subject to pre‑emption, provided such transfer receives Board approval and is reported to the Libro de Socios and Registro Mercantil.

## 5. Board Meetings, Quorum, and Voting
- Quorum: validly constituted with the attendance (in person or telematic means) of a majority of directors.
- Majority: resolutions by simple majority of directors present or represented.
- Casting Vote (Voto de Calidad): In case of a tie, the Chair shall have a casting vote to break the tie, subject to the restrictions detailed in the notarial clause below and in LSC Articles 228-230.
- Restrictions on Casting Vote: The Chair cannot exercise the casting vote when: (i) the Chair has a conflict of interest; (ii) the matter concerns Reserved Matters requiring reinforced majorities; (iii) the matter relates to the Chair's own appointment, removal, or compensation; (iv) the matter involves related-party transactions in which the Chair has direct or indirect interest; or (v) exercising the casting vote would breach the Chair's fiduciary duties.
- Escalation: If the casting vote cannot be exercised, the matter is deferred to the next Board meeting. If deadlock persists after good-faith redeliberation, the matter escalates to: (a) an Independent Director, or (b) the General Meeting of Shareholders, or (c) mediation under Madrid Chamber of Commerce Rules, as detailed in Section 9 and the notarial clause below.

## 6. General Meeting

## 7. Reserved Matters (Materias Reservadas)
Board and/or General Meeting matters requiring enhanced approval: CEO appointment/removal, annual budget > EUR [•], equity issuances, amendments to Class A/B rights, IP assignments outside the group, amendments to these Bylaws.

## 8. IP Ownership
La propiedad intelectual y las obligaciones de cesión se rigen por contratos escritos separados entre la Sociedad y sus empleados, contratistas, administradores y directivos, y por acuerdos intragrupo con Ai‑First Holdings Ltd. (Cayman). Todos los empleados y contratistas deberán suscribir contratos de cesión de propiedad intelectual como condición de su contratación, respetando los derechos morales inalienables conforme al Real Decreto Legislativo 1/1996.

## 9. Deadlock Resolution
If a deadlock persists for five (5) Business Days after good‑faith negotiation on a Reserved Matter, the Chair's casting vote resolves the specific matter. If the Chair is conflicted, escalate to an Independent Director within five (5) Business Days; failing appointment of an Independent Director or resolution within five (5) Business Days thereafter, escalate to mediation under the Madrid Chamber of Commerce Rules.

## 10. Governing Law and Jurisdiction
Spanish law. Disputes subject to the courts of Madrid, Spain, without prejudice to arbitration clauses agreed by the shareholders.

## 11. Data Protection Compliance

**GDPR and Spanish Law Compliance**: The Company shall comply with Regulation (EU) 2016/679 (GDPR) and Spanish Organic Law 3/2018 on the Protection of Personal Data and Guarantee of Digital Rights.

**Responsibilities**: The Board shall appoint a person responsible for data protection compliance and shall adopt policies and procedures implementing:

- Lawful bases for data processing (GDPR Art. 6)
- Technical and organizational security measures (GDPR Art. 32)
- Data subject rights procedures (GDPR Art. 15-22)
- Data breach notification protocols (GDPR Art. 33-34)
- Data Processing Agreements with third-party processors (GDPR Art. 28)
- Cross-border data transfer mechanisms (GDPR Art. 44-50)
- Records of processing activities (GDPR Art. 30)

**Board Oversight**: The Board shall review data protection compliance quarterly and engage external audit annually.

**Data Protection Officer**: The Board shall determine whether appointment of a Data Protection Officer is required under GDPR Art. 37 based on the nature, scope, and purposes of data processing activities.

## 12. External Audit

**Audit Requirement**: The Company shall appoint external auditors (auditores de cuentas) when required under Article 263 of the Ley de Sociedades de Capital. Pursuant to LSC Article 263, the Company is required to appoint auditors if, at the close of the fiscal year, it meets at least two of the following three thresholds on a consolidated basis (if applicable) or individual basis:

1. Total assets exceeding EUR 2,850,000;
2. Net turnover (cifra de negocios) exceeding EUR 5,700,000;
3. Average number of employees during the fiscal year exceeding 50.

**Appointment and Term**: External auditors shall be appointed by the General Meeting for an initial term of three (3) years, extendable for additional periods of three years each, in accordance with LSC Articles 264-266 and Ley 22/2015 de Auditoría de Cuentas. The Board shall propose the appointment of auditors to the General Meeting and ensure that appointed auditors are registered with the Registro Oficial de Auditores de Cuentas (ROAC).

**Independence and Scope**: The appointed auditors must be independent in accordance with LSC Article 264 and Ley 22/2015. The audit shall cover the annual accounts (cuentas anuales) comprising the balance sheet, profit and loss statement, statement of changes in equity, cash flow statement, and notes to the financial statements (memoria), and shall result in an audit report (informe de auditoría) in accordance with Spanish GAAP or IFRS as adopted by the EU.

**Voluntary Audit**: Even if not required under LSC Article 263, the Board may propose to the General Meeting the voluntary appointment of external auditors to enhance financial transparency, facilitate financing, or meet investor requirements.

### Cláusula Notarial — Voto de Calidad (Texto en Español)

«En caso de empate en cualquier votación del Consejo de Administración, siempre que el Consejo esté válidamente constituido con el quórum exigido, el Presidente ostentará voto de calidad exclusivamente para dirimir dicho empate.

El voto de calidad del Presidente no podrá ejercerse en los siguientes supuestos:

(i) Cuando el Presidente se halle incurso en conflicto de interés conforme a los artículos 228 a 230 de la Ley de Sociedades de Capital, incluyendo sin carácter limitativo: (a) operaciones vinculadas en las que el Presidente, sus familiares hasta el segundo grado, o sociedades en las que ostente participación significativa tengan interés directo o indirecto; (b) situaciones en las que el Presidente, por cuenta propia o ajena, haya realizado, realice o pretenda realizar una actividad que entrañe competencia efectiva con la Sociedad; (c) aprovechamiento de oportunidades de negocio de la Sociedad;

(ii) Respecto de Materias Reservadas que requieran mayoría reforzada conforme a estos Estatutos o al Pacto de Socios, incluyendo sin carácter limitativo: aprobación de presupuestos que excedan los límites establecidos, emisiones de participaciones o valores convertibles, modificación de derechos de las distintas clases de participaciones, transmisiones o gravámenes de propiedad intelectual esencial fuera del curso ordinario de los negocios, y modificación de estos Estatutos;

(iii) En asuntos relativos a la designación, ratificación, reelección, cese, remoción, o retribución del propio Presidente;

(iv) En operaciones vinculadas en las que el Presidente tenga interés directo o indirecto, según lo establecido en los artículos 228 a 230 LSC y en la normativa de desarrollo;

(v) En cualquier otra materia en la que el ejercicio del voto de calidad pueda suponer un conflicto de interés o vulnerar los deberes fiduciarios del Presidente conforme a la Ley de Sociedades de Capital.

En caso de que el voto de calidad no pueda ejercerse por concurrir alguna de las circunstancias anteriores, el Consejo no adoptará resolución respecto del asunto objeto de empate, quedando diferido hasta la siguiente sesión del Consejo de Administración. Si en la siguiente sesión del Consejo persistiera el empate tras nueva deliberación de buena fe, el asunto se someterá a:

(a) Decisión de un Consejero Independiente designado por acuerdo de los consejeros no conflictuados, si lo hubiere; o en su defecto,

(b) Decisión de la Junta General de Socios, con las mayorías previstas en estos Estatutos o en el Pacto de Socios para la materia de que se trate; o en su defecto,

(c) Mediación conforme al Reglamento de Mediación de la Cámara de Comercio de Madrid, siendo vinculante el acuerdo alcanzado mediante mediación.

El ejercicio del voto de calidad por el Presidente se hará constar expresamente en el acta del Consejo de Administración, junto con la manifestación del Presidente de que no concurre ninguna de las circunstancias excluyentes anteriormente señaladas.»

### Cláusula Notarial — Transmisiones bajo Pacto de Socios
«Las transmisiones de participaciones sociales que se realicen en ejecución de mecanismos de compraventa entre socios (incluyendo cláusula ‘shotgun’ o salida a valor de mercado) previstos en un pacto de socios debidamente suscrito por todos los socios, se consideran transmisiones permitidas a los efectos estatutarios, siempre que se cumplan las formalidades de comunicación y, cuando proceda, se obtengan las autorizaciones exigidas por la Ley de Sociedades de Capital.»
