### 9.1 Data Processing Addendum — Contractor Data

This Section 9.1 governs the processing and international transfer of Contractor personal data for contractor administration, compliance, and project management purposes.

**(a) Data Controllers and Roles**:
- **Primary Controller**: Ai First Inc. ("Company"), acting as the primary data controller for Contractor data.
- **Related Controllers**: Ai‑First Holdings Ltd. (Cayman Islands) ("Cayman HoldCo") acts as independent controller with respect to consolidated Group reporting, vendor administration, and intercompany coordination.

**(b) Scope of Personal Data**: This Section covers all personal data relating to Contractor (individual or entity representative) processed by Company and disclosed to Cayman HoldCo or other Group entities, including without limitation:
- (i) **Identification Data**: Full name, Social Security Number (or EIN/TIN for entities), address, email, phone;
- (ii) **Billing and Payment Data**: Invoices, rates, bank details (ACH/wire information), tax withholding (Form W-9, 1099-NEC), payment records;
- (iii) **Performance Data**: Deliverables, timelines, work acceptance, quality evaluations, project communications;
- (iv) **IT Data**: Temporary corporate email or system access (if provided), access logs to Company systems.

**(c) Purposes**: Company processes Contractor data for:
- (i) **Contractor Administration and Payments**: Invoice processing, payment execution, tax reporting (Form 1099-NEC filing with IRS), compliance with federal and state tax law;
- (ii) **Project Management and Compliance**: Tracking deliverables, quality control, vendor performance evaluation, internal audit;
- (iii) **IT Security**: Provisioning temporary system access, monitoring access for security and policy compliance, incident response;
- (iv) **Group Consolidation and Reporting**: Consolidated vendor spend and project reporting to Cayman HoldCo Board, Group-wide vendor analytics;
- (v) **Legal Claims and Regulatory Compliance**: Responding to government inquiries, litigation, insurance claims, record retention per applicable law.

**(d) International Transfers**: Where Contractor data is transferred from the United States to Cayman Islands or other jurisdictions outside the United States:
- (i) Company shall implement appropriate safeguards, including contractual protections and technical and organizational measures (encryption in transit and at rest, access controls, MFA, audit logging, annual security audits);
- (ii) Data recipients in Cayman Islands or other jurisdictions shall implement data protection obligations equivalent to those in this Section, including obligations to: maintain confidentiality; implement security measures appropriate to risk; notify Company of any data breach within 24 hours; cooperate with Contractor data subject rights requests; and return or delete data upon request or termination of services.

**(e) Data Subject Rights**: Contractor (or individual representatives of entity Contractors) may request access to, correction of, or deletion of Contractor's personal data held by Company or Cayman HoldCo by contacting [privacy@aifirst.example]. Company will respond to such requests within 30 days (or as required by applicable state privacy law, e.g., California CCPA/CPRA within 45 days). Contractor acknowledges that certain data must be retained for legal compliance (tax, audit, litigation) and cannot be deleted while such obligations remain.

**(f) Security Breach Notification**: In the event of a data breach affecting Contractor personal data, Company shall notify Contractor in accordance with applicable federal and state data breach notification laws, including without limitation state laws requiring notification within specified timeframes.

**(g) Data Retention**: Company shall retain Contractor data for the term of the services contract and thereafter for applicable statutory limitation periods (IRS Form 1099 records: 4 years from filing; state tax: varies; commercial records: 6 years; litigation: statute of limitations), after which data will be deleted or anonymized.

**(h) State-Specific Privacy Rights**: For Contractors who are residents of states with comprehensive consumer privacy laws (California, Colorado, Connecticut, Utah, Virginia, etc.), the following additional rights may apply:
- (i) **Right to Know/Access**: Right to request disclosure of categories and specific pieces of personal data collected, sources, purposes, and third parties with whom data is shared;
- (ii) **Right to Delete**: Right to request deletion of personal data, subject to exceptions for legal compliance, fraud prevention, and internal uses;
- (iii) **Right to Correct**: Right to request correction of inaccurate personal data;
- (iv) **Right to Opt-Out of Sale/Sharing**: Company does not sell or share Contractor personal data for cross-context behavioral advertising; if this changes, Contractor will be notified and given opportunity to opt out;
- (v) **No Discrimination**: Company shall not discriminate against Contractor for exercising privacy rights.

To exercise state-specific privacy rights, contact [privacy@aifirst.example] or use the online form at [privacy request URL]. Company will respond within the timeframes required by applicable law (typically 45 days, with one 45-day extension if needed).

**(i) GDPR Protections for EU/EEA Residents**: If Contractor is a resident of the European Union or European Economic Area (regardless of work location), Contractor is entitled to the protections of the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), including: right of access (Article 15); right to rectification (Article 16); right to erasure (Article 17); right to restriction of processing (Article 18); right to data portability (Article 20); right to object (Article 21); right not to be subject to automated decision-making (Article 22); and right to lodge a complaint with a supervisory authority. Company shall comply with such requests in accordance with GDPR. For transfers of EU/EEA resident data outside the EU/EEA, Company shall implement Standard Contractual Clauses or other appropriate safeguards under GDPR Article 46.

**(j) Third-Party Processors**: Company may engage third-party service providers (payment processors, accounting software providers, IT service providers, cloud infrastructure providers) to process Contractor personal data on Company's behalf. Company shall ensure that such processors are contractually bound to data protection obligations equivalent to those in this Section and shall remain responsible for processor performance.

**(k) Survival**: The obligations in this Section 9.1 shall survive termination of the services contract for so long as Company or Cayman HoldCo processes or retains Contractor personal data, and in any event for applicable statutory limitation periods under federal and state law.
